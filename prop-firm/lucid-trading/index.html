Lucid Trading Overview
Everything you need to know at a glance.
🇺🇸 United States CEO: AJ Campanella Founded 2025

Lucid Trading launched in early 2025 and has quickly become one of the fastest-growing futures prop firms, known for end-of-day (EOD) trailing drawdown, free account activation, and payouts that are typically processed in around 15 minutes. It runs three programs — LucidPro, LucidFlex, and LucidDirect — which share the same EOD drawdown system, instrument list, platform ecosystem, and 90/10 profit split. The firm operates under Lucid Prop Ltd and was founded by AJ Campanella.

Lucid Trading is best for traders who want flexibility. The LucidFlex path removes the daily loss limit entirely — in both evaluation and the funded account — the closest thing to unrestricted prop trading in the futures space. LucidDirect lets experienced traders skip the evaluation and trade a funded account from day one. LucidPro keeps the fastest payout cadence, with a daily loss limit from $50K and a 40% consistency check once funded.

Expert Review
In-depth review by Jered Klima, Editor in Chief, based on extensive hands-on testing.
If you want the fastest, most reliable path to a real funded futures account in 2026, Lucid Trading is the firm to beat — full stop.
— Jered Klima, Editor in Chief

Lucid Trading is the rare prop firm that lives up to its marketing. Across LucidPro, LucidFlex, and LucidDirect, payouts arrived in around 15 minutes of approval. That's not normal. Most firms in this space promise fast payouts and deliver slow ones. Lucid reverses the pattern, and it backs it with end-of-day trailing drawdown so a single intraday spike can't end your account.

LucidPro Accounts: where most traders should start

Their LucidPro Accounts are designed for traders who want to scale gradually. The 1-step evaluation can be passed in as little as one trading day, with no consistency rule during the evaluation phase. This is among the most permissive eval structures in the industry. The Dynamic Daily Loss Limit guardrail prevents catastrophic losses while you're learning the firm's rhythm. For traders new to prop firms, this is a much gentler on-ramp than Topstep's Trading Combine.

LucidFlex: the standout for funded traders

For my own trading, I run LucidFlex. The no-DLL funded environment changes how you can manage positions through news, gap openings, and volatile sessions. It's the closest thing to trading without a safety net in a funded environment — which sounds scarier than it is, because you still have the EOD trailing drawdown floor underneath you at all times. The 40% consistency rule during evaluation disappears entirely once you're funded.

Lightning: powerful but unforgiving

For experienced traders ready to skip evaluation, LucidDirect is the standout offering. Pay a one-time fee, bypass traditional evaluations, and start trading live capital immediately. But here's the thing most reviews don't mention loudly enough: Lightning has no resets. A breached account is a dead account. You purchase again at full price. The 20% consistency rule on the first payout is also tighter than most traders expect on a first read.

Where Lucid Trading isn't perfect

The 20% consistency rule on LucidDirect is the main friction point. If you have one big winning day, that win caps how much you can profit on other days. Hedging and Martingale are also not permitted, which closes off some strategies that other firms allow. And there's no refund policy — if you change your mind after purchase, you're stuck with the account.

But for the typical futures trader — someone who wants to pass an evaluation once, get funded, and start collecting consistent payouts — Lucid Trading is the most direct route there is. Its combination of competitive pricing, market-leading split, and a payout track record that actually delivers is hard to beat.

What you need to know

Four key rules that govern how eligibility works in practice.

🏠
Citizenship and residency
Lucid screens on both your citizenship and your country of residence. If either is on the restricted list you are ineligible — e.g. a Nigerian citizen living in the US is still restricted, and a US citizen residing in a restricted country is too.
✈
Travel is OK
If you're a resident of an eligible country but traveling to or vacationing in a restricted country, you can continue trading normally. Restrictions apply to permanent residence, not current location.
🚫
No VPN bypass
Using a VPN to access Lucid Trading from a restricted country is a hard breach. Lucid Trading uses IP logging and pattern detection — sustained VPN use results in account closure and forfeiture of profits.
📄
KYC required
All funded traders complete KYC verification: government photo ID plus proof of address dated within 90 days. Processing takes 24–48 hours, sometimes same-day.

General FAQ
The most common questions traders ask about Lucid Trading, across all account types.
How fast does Lucid Trading actually pay out?

Payouts are processed quickly — typically in around 15 minutes after approval. All funded accounts run a 90/10 profit split. Exact timing can vary with your payment provider (Lucid uses providers such as Rise and Plane).

What's the difference between LucidPro, LucidFlex, and LucidDirect?

LucidPro is a 1-step evaluation with the fastest payout cadence; it carries a daily loss limit from $50K up and a 40% consistency rule once funded. LucidFlex is a 1-step evaluation with no daily loss limit at all and no consistency rule once funded (50% applies during the evaluation only). LucidDirect skips the evaluation entirely — you're funded instantly but face a 20% consistency rule on every payout and no resets.

What's the consistency rule and how does it work?

The consistency rule limits how much of your total profit can come from a single trading day when you request a payout. On LucidDirect, a 20% rule applies on every payout. On LucidPro funded accounts the rule is 40% on every payout. LucidFlex applies 50% during the evaluation only and has no consistency rule once funded.

What platforms does Lucid Trading support?

All platforms are included free. Via the CQG feed: NinjaTrader, Tradovate, and TradingView. Via the Rithmic feed: Quantower, MotiveWave, Sierra Chart, Bookmap, Jigsaw, ATAS, R Trader Pro, MultiCharts, and Tradesea.

Can I trade news events on Lucid Trading?

Yes — Lucid Trading allows news trading without restriction, plus scaling/DCA and automated and copy trading. Hedging is prohibited on all account types (and by CME rules on live accounts).

Can I hold positions overnight or over the weekend?

No. Overnight and weekend holding is not permitted on any Lucid Trading account, regardless of program. All positions must be closed by 4:45 PM EST each trading day.

How many funded accounts can I have?

Up to 5 funded accounts per trader, with a combined maximum balance of $750,000. You can mix and match across LucidPro, LucidFlex, and LucidDirect. Each account is treated independently for drawdown, daily loss limits, and consistency calculations.

What happens if I breach my account?

For LucidPro and LucidFlex accounts, resets are available — you can purchase a reset to continue. LucidDirect accounts have no resets. A breached LucidDirect account is permanently dead and you'd need to purchase a new one at full price.

Is Lucid Trading legit?

Yes. Lucid Trading (Lucid Prop Ltd) is a US-based futures prop firm founded by AJ Campanella in 2025. It has quickly built a strong reputation for EOD trailing drawdown and fast (~15-minute) payouts, with a roughly 4.6–4.7 trader rating across 4,000+ reviews.
