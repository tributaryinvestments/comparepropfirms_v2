Funded Futures Network Overview
Everything you need to know at a glance.
🇺🇸 United States Founded 2022 New York, NY 👤 CEO: Kevin Swart

Funded Futures Network (FFN) is a US-based futures prop firm founded in 2022 and led by CEO Kevin Swart, operating as Funded Futures Network LLC out of New York. It uses a simple model — Evaluation → (Exhibition, on OG) → Funded — built around two products, Standard and Express, each offered in an OG (with Exhibition) and a MAX (no Exhibition) style. An Exhibition is a short stage after the evaluation — used only on OG plans — where you trade to build a profit buffer before your account becomes funded. The firm is known for a forgiving realized/EOD trailing drawdown, same-day no-fee payouts, and aggressive promotional pricing.

FFN suits disciplined intraday futures traders. The Standard product gives you more days to pass at a lower price, while Express funds you faster on a tighter schedule. Within each, the OG style adds an Exhibition stage before withdrawals from funding where you build a funded buffer first and the MAX style skips it, building your funded account without a cap initially. The profit split is 80/20 while sim-funded and improves to 90/10 once you reach a live-funded account.

The 30-second verdict

Want the fastest route to funded? Take an Express account (2–4 days to pass). Prefer more breathing room at a lower price? Take a Standard account (5–7 days). Choose MAX if you’d rather skip the Exhibition stage and build your account without a capped buffer inside the funded account; choose OG if you prefer a classic Exhibition step first where you build the buffer and then move to a funded account that allows for withdraws. Expert Review
In-depth review by the CPF editorial team, based on hands-on testing of all four plans.
For traders who want choice in how they get funded — two products, two evaluation styles and five sizes — Funded Futures Network is one of the most flexible options in 2026, with a forgiving drawdown and same-day, no-fee payouts.
— CPF Editorial

Funded Futures Network launched in 2022 under CEO Kevin Swart and has built its reputation on flexibility and trader-friendly mechanics rather than gimmicks. The headline value is a forgiving realized/EOD trailing drawdown, same-day payouts with no fees, and a lineup that lets you pick exactly how you want to get funded: Standard or Express, each in an OG or MAX style.

Two products, two styles

Standard gives you more days to pass (5–7 depending on style) at a lower price; Express funds you faster on a tighter 2–4 day schedule. Within each, OG adds an Exhibition stage where you build your buffer before reaching a funded account, while MAX skips Exhibition and builds the buffer inside the funded account. With the current BOGOISBACK promo (50% off + Buy One Get One + $0 activation), a 50K Standard MAX works out to an effective $40.

Forgiving drawdown, fast payouts

The OG styles use an intraday trailing drawdown that only moves when you close a trade in profit, and it converts to a near-static limit once funded. Standard MAX uses an end-of-day drawdown with a soft daily-loss lockout instead of a hard fail. Consistency rules vary by plan (25–50%). Payouts are processed the same day via ACH, PayPal or wire with no fees, $500 minimum. A $126/month data fee applies on live-funded accounts only (not sim-funded). A reset costs $100.

Where FFN isn't perfect

It is strictly day-trading only — positions must be flat by 4:50 PM EST, with the session reopening at 6:00 PM EST. The sim-funded split is 80/20 until you reach a live-funded account (which requires $5,000 in cumulative payouts), and sim accounts carry per-account payout caps that scale with size. The four-way plan matrix also takes a moment to learn, and the Trustpilot sample is smaller than the largest firms.

For a disciplined intraday futures trader who values choice in evaluation style, a forgiving drawdown and fast, no-fee payouts, FFN is a strong pick for 2026. If you want a flat 90% split from day one or an instant-funding option, compare it against Topstep or Tradeify first.

General FAQ
The most common questions traders ask about Funded Futures Network, across all four plans.
What account products does FFN offer?

Two products — Standard and Express — each offered in an OG (with Exhibition) and a MAX (no Exhibition) style, across five sizes ($25K / $50K / $100K / $150K / $250K). Standard gives you more days to pass at a lower price; Express funds you faster. Profit targets, drawdown and contract caps scale with size.

How does the FFN evaluation work?

You pass an Evaluation (hit the profit target while respecting the drawdown and the plan's consistency rule). On OG plans you then clear a short Exhibition stage to build your buffer; on MAX plans you skip Exhibition and build the buffer inside the funded account. Funded accounts are sim-funded at an 80/20 split; once you reach $5,000 in cumulative payouts you can move to a live-funded account at 90/10 with no caps and no consistency rule.

How and when does FFN pay out?

Payouts are processed the same day, on demand, with a $500 minimum and a $10,000 per-user cap. To qualify you build your buffer, meet the plan's consistency rule (25–50% depending on plan) and keep your balance at least $500 above the buffer; Express MAX also needs 5 winning days. Funds arrive via ACH, PayPal or bank wire with no fees. A $126/month data fee applies on live-funded accounts only (not sim-funded). The sim split is 80/20, improving to 90/10 on live-funded accounts.

How many accounts can I run at once?

You can hold up to 10 accounts total with up to 5 funded at once; additional accounts queue and activate as funded ones close. You can copy trade across your 5 funded accounts — roughly $1.25M in combined capital. Everything sits under one FFN login.

Can I trade news, scalp, or hold overnight?

News trading is allowed during the evaluation. On exhibition and funded accounts you must be flat one minute before and after Tier-1 events (FOMC, CPI, employment; plus EIA for energy traders and key agricultural reports for ag traders). Scalping, DCA and copy trading (across your 5 funded accounts) are allowed; cross-account hedging is prohibited. It is day-trading only: all positions flat by 4:50 PM EST, session reopens 6:00 PM EST.

What's the difference between OG and MAX?

OG accounts include an Exhibition stage after the evaluation, where you build your buffer before reaching a funded account, and use an intraday realized trailing drawdown. MAX accounts skip Exhibition — you pass and go straight to funded, building the buffer inside the funded account; Standard MAX uses an end-of-day drawdown with a soft daily-loss lockout. Both come on the Standard and Express products, so there are four combinations in total.

What platforms and data feed does FFN use?

Trade on FFN’s free proprietary platforms EdgeProX and FundX, or connect Quantower, NinjaTrader or Sierra Chart (NinjaTrader and Sierra Chart require your own license). Execution runs on Rithmic. A $126/month data fee applies on live-funded accounts only (not sim-funded). You trade CME Group futures and their micros.

Is FFN legit?

FFN operates as Funded Futures Network LLC, founded in 2022 and based in New York, led by CEO Kevin Swart. Its public Trustpilot score is around 4.6/5 from roughly 426 reviews — strong on payout speed and support. As always, read the rulebook — particularly the per-plan drawdown, consistency and news rules — before purchasing.
